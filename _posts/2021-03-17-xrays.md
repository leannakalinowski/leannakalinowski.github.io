Yesterday was my follow-up appointment and I saw my x-rays for the first time. (CW: the x-rays are gnarly)

I wasn't going to blog about it because it wasn't too eventful, but as soon as I saw the x-rays, I knew I had to share them everywhere.

Everything is looking good so far. My cast was taken off, my staples were taken out (all 35 of them - ouch!), and I was given a walking boot that I'm allowed to take off whenever I'm laying around (which is pretty much 23 hours per day). Next appointment is April 13th, where I'll hopefully get the green light to start PT and learn how to walk again.

**Now for the x-rays...**
I'll start with the one from immediately after surgery so that you have a reference point for what an ankle is supposed to look like (minus the half-built Eiffel Tower of hardware).

![post surgery xray showing my hardware](/assets/161268634_3780432365385648_3963378339488625909_n.jpg)

And then here's what my ankle looked like immediately after the fall! Note the totally dislocated ankle, the snapped fibula, and the crazy separation between the tibia and fibula. You can tell how much pressure was being put onto my skin by the end of the tibia... I'm amazed that it didn't go through. 

![pre surgery xray showing how bad the break is](/assets/161858650_3780432348718983_4644082326101631804_n.jpg)

Yeah, IMPRESSIVE right? I want to include them in my dissertation acknowledgements once I get to that point 10 years from now (jk, jk).
Last injury blog while I wait in limbo until April 13th!
