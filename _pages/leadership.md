## Current Roles
### APA Science Student Council
The APA Science Student Council advocates for the needs of research-oriented graduate students in psychology, and I am particularly charged with focusing on issues that impact behavioral neuroscience students.

Behavioral Neuroscience Representative (2020-2022):
- Conduct reviews for the APA Early Graduate Student Researcher Award
- Write newsletters that focus on graduate student issues (Fall 2021: disconnecting from technology in a technology-focused world)
- Advocate for federal funding of psychological research and funding through meetings with congressional leaders (May 2021: "Stand for Science to Advance Psychology" Advocacy Summit)
- Make recommendations to the APA for student programming and DEI initiatives

Chair (2021-2022):
- Chair-elect/in training until my term begins on 9/1
- Organize and facilite monthly council meetings
- Collaborate with APA staff to develop new projects and initiatives for the council
- Represent the council by attending and reporting back on semi-annual APA Board of Scientific Affairs meetings
- Coordinate administrative tasks for the Early Graduate Student Researcher Award application review
- Communicate with other APA student groups to coordinate shared activities (e.g., APAGS Science & Convention Committees)

### American Psychological Association of Graduate Students (APAGS)
Science Committee Member (2019-2021):
- Conduct annual grant reviews for two major awards (APAGS Psychological Science Research Grant & APA/Psi Chi Junior Scientist Fellowship)
- Promote psychological science through blog writing
- Organize and facilitate programming for the annual APA convention (2021: workshops on peer reviewing as a graduate student & advocating for graduate student debt relief)
- Create special topics for APAGS' journal, Translational Issues in Psychological Science (TPS), by forecasting critical social issues to which psychology research could be applied (2023: The Brain-body Connection)
- Serve as a Guest Associate Editor for the COVID-19 special issue of TPS (to be published September 2022)
- Work with the APAGS committee at large to reconstruct review criteria for the APAGS Student Diversity Initiative Award
- Draft comments on behalf of APAGS for the Committee on Animal Research and Ethics (CARE) Guidelines for Ethical Conduct in the Care and Use of Nonhuman Animals in Research & the Committee on Human Research (CHR) Guidelines for Ethical Conduct of Behavioral Projects Involving Human Participants by High School Students

### Toronto Science Policy Network
The Toronto Science Policy Network is a student-run group which provides a platform to learn more about and engage in science policy. Activities include events centered upon science policy (e.g., talks, workshops, panels) and advocacy. 

Volunteer (2021):
- Managed the newsletter sent out to all TSPN members biweekly
- Served on the Knowledge Translation Workshop planning team (June 2021)

Internal Affairs Director (2021-2022):
- Coordinate organizational recruitment efforts and manage volunteers
- Maintain and manage organizational listservs, newsletters, meetings, Slack channels, and event calendars
- Handle official correspondence of the organization in coordination with the co-presidents
- Monitor email and respond to inquiries
